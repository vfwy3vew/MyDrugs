What is MDMA?

MDMA (also known as Molly or Ecstasy) is a synthetic substance that alters mood and perception. It increases the activity of serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine in the brain.

Before You Use

    Avoid mixing with other substances like alcohol or stimulants.
    Know your dosage: A standard beginner dose is around 75–100mg. Avoid redosing.

During Use

    Take breaks from dancing to avoid overheating.
    Watch for warning signs like nausea, confusion, rapid heartbeat, or overheating.
    Be around trusted friends and in a safe, cool environment.

Aftercare (Comedown)

    Expect mild mood dips for 1–3 days.
    Eat healthy food, sleep well, and avoid stress.
    Avoid using MDMA again for at least 4–6 weeks to let your serotonin levels recover.

Warning Signs of Danger

    High body temperature
    Seizures
    Fainting
    Severe agitation or hallucinations
